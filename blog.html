Travel Experiences

Arriving in Hong Kong for the first time is an overwhelming but exciting experience. The city is alive at all hours – the neon signs, the bustling markets, and the incredible food stalls make every street feel like an adventure. The contrast between the modern skyscrapers and the traditional temples is remarkable.

Taking the Star Ferry across Victoria Harbour at night, with the city lights reflecting on the water, is one of the most memorable travel moments I have had. It perfectly captures what makes Hong Kong so unique – old and new existing side by side.

Travel Tips

Get an Octopus card as soon as you arrive – it works on the MTR, buses, trams, and even in many shops and restaurants. It saves time and money.

Visit the local dai pai dong (open-air food stalls) for affordable and authentic food. The Temple Street Night Market is great for street food and souvenirs.

Avoid visiting during July and August if possible – the heat and humidity can be intense. October to December is the most comfortable time of year.

Destination Highlights

Victoria Peak – the views of the skyline from the top are breathtaking, especially at dusk when the lights begin to come on across the harbour.

Lantau Island – home to the Giant Buddha and the charming fishing village of Tai O, this island offers a peaceful escape from the busy city.
